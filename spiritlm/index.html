Interleaved Spoken and Written Language Model


We introduce Spirit-LM, a collection of foundation multimodal language models that freely mixes text and speech, and are trained jointly with tokens extracted from both speech and text corpora. The speech tokens are based on speech units independently capturing semantics, pitch, and style information, while the text is encoded with subword BPE tokens. They are concatenated as a single set of tokens, and trained with a word-level interleaving method using a small automatically-curated speech-text parallel corpus. The resulting models display both the semantic abilities of text models and the expressive abilities of speech models. Additionally, we demonstrate that SPIRIT-LM is able to learn new tasks in a few-shot fashion across modalities (i.e. ASR, TTS, Speech Classification).
